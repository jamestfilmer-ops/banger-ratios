# About Me — James Filmer

## Who I Am

My name is James Filmer. I'm a **Certified Financial Planner (CFP)** based in **Nashville, Tennessee**. I run my own financial planning practice and I'm building Banger Ratios as a side project — partly because I love music and data, and partly because the financial advisory industry is under real pressure from automation and fee compression, and I need to build something that can become its own income stream.

I am a husband and a new dad (newborn daughter). My time is genuinely limited. I work on this project in whatever pockets of time I can find — sometimes 20 minutes, sometimes a few hours on a weekend. I need to be able to stop and pick up exactly where I left off without re-explaining everything.

I am a Christian. My values shape how I work and what I build. I want Banger Ratios to be something I'm proud of — honest, useful, and not built on manipulation or dark patterns.

## My Technical Reality

**I have zero coding experience.** I am not a developer. I do not know what most code means line by line. What I can do:

- Open Terminal and run commands I'm given
- Open VS Code, press Cmd+A, delete, and paste new code
- Follow step-by-step guides if they're written clearly
- Recognize when something is broken vs. working
- Spot when output doesn't match what was described

I learn by doing and by having things explained in plain English. I don't need to know *why* code works — I need to know *what to do* and *what to expect*.

## My Working Pattern

- I work in short sessions — 20 minutes to 2 hours
- I always come back to this project after breaks of hours or days
- I use Cowork and Claude.ai Projects so context persists
- When something breaks, I copy the exact error message and ask for help
- I do NOT push code to GitHub until `npm run build` passes

## My Goals for This Project

**Short term:** Get bangerratios.com to a state where I can show it to people and be proud of it. Working features, no crashes, real data.

**Medium term:** Build the things that make investors and music industry people take it seriously — the /data page, public stats, share cards, activity feed.

**Long term:** Turn this into something that generates real revenue. Either through a Pro subscription tier, music industry B2B licensing of the rating data, or brand partnerships. This is a backup plan for a profession that may not survive the next decade intact.

## My Relationship with AI

I use Claude as my developer. I don't resent this — I think it's the rational play. I'm not going to become a programmer at 30-something with a newborn. What I *can* do is direct, make product decisions, and deploy. That's the division of labor I've accepted. I trust Claude to write good code. I trust myself to know when something feels wrong or off-brand.

## Key Fact for Every Session

I am the only person building this. There is no team. Every decision runs through me.
